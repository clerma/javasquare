A · Main floor, looking east toward the vault # ──────────── CHAPTER 1 ──────────── ch1_label: § 01 ch1_title: The building ch1_lede: >- 103 West Landry was built in the 1920s as a bank. It held a town's savings through booms, busts, a hurricane or two, and most of a century of Louisiana Tuesdays. ch1_body_1: >- By the time we found it, the marble counters were dusty, the vault door hadn't moved in decades, and the old teller windows were boarded over. We kept the bones: the crown molding, the iron gates, the vault itself. We painted the walls cream, the cabinets navy, and the trim a quiet brown that matches the logo. ch1_body_2: >- Everyone asks the same question — can we sit in the vault? Yes. There are four seats inside. They book up fast on Saturdays. # ──────────── SPLIT IMAGES ──────────── split_image_1: https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1497515114629-f71d768fd07c?w=900&auto=format&fit=crop&q=80 split_image_2: https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1554118811-1e0d58224f24?w=900&auto=format&fit=crop&q=80 # ──────────── CHAPTER 2 ──────────── ch2_label: § 02 ch2_title: The coffee ch2_lede: >- Beans from local roasters, poured by people who genuinely care what yours tastes like. ch2_body_1: >- We buy from roasters we've visited. We dial in every morning. We taste the first shot before the doors open, and we re-dial when the weather changes — because humidity changes everything and pretending otherwise is how you get a bitter cup. ch2_body_2: >- Our drip is a medium roast with caramel and red fruit notes. The espresso runs darker, built for milk drinks. Cold brew steeps twelve hours. Everything else — the blends, the seasonals, the Fredda recipe — we tweak until it's right and then we leave it alone. # ──────────── VALUES ──────────── values_eyebrow: § 03 — What we believe values: - heading: Slower is better. body: >- Nobody ever walked out of a great conversation wishing they'd rushed. Our bar doesn't have a pickup window. - heading: Local over far. body: >- Boudin from down the street. Pastries baked before sunrise by people whose names we know. Beans from roasters we've shaken hands with. - heading: A room, not a transaction. body: >- Bring your laptop, your date, your grandmother, your book club. Stay as long as a single coffee will let you. We don't time-limit the Wi-Fi. - heading: Opelousas, specifically. body: >- We're not trying to be the Opelousas version of a Brooklyn cafe. We're trying to be a great cafe that could only exist here. # ──────────── END CTA ──────────── end_title: Come meet us. end_body: Downtown Opelousas, across from the murals.
